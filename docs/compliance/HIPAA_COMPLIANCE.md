## 1. Privacy Policy

### HealthPulse Pro Privacy Policy

**Effective Date**: [Date]

**Introduction**

HealthPulse Pro ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your Protected Health Information (PHI). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your health information in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations.

**Scope**

This policy applies to all PHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted by HealthPulse Pro in any form (electronic, paper, or oral).

**Definitions**

- **Protected Health Information (PHI)**: Individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form
- **Covered Entity**: Healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses
- **Business Associate**: Person or organization that performs functions involving PHI on behalf of a covered entity

### Information We Collect

**1. Health Information**
- Medical history and diagnoses
- Vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, etc.)
- Laboratory test results
- Prescription information
- Clinical notes and observations
- Wearable device data (if connected)

**2. Demographic Information**
- Name, date of birth, gender
- Contact information (address, phone, email)
- Emergency contact information
- Insurance information

**3. Technical Information**
- IP address and device information (for security purposes)
- Usage data and analytics (de-identified when possible)
- Authentication and access logs

### How We Use Your Information

We use your PHI for the following purposes, as permitted by HIPAA:

**1. Treatment**
- Providing clinical care and decision support
- AI-powered analysis to identify health risks
- Coordinating care with your healthcare providers
- Generating clinical alerts for your care team

**2. Payment**
- Processing billing and reimbursement
- Verifying insurance coverage
- Claims management

**3. Healthcare Operations**
- Quality improvement and patient safety
- Training healthcare professionals
- Internal auditing and compliance
- Business planning and development

**4. Research (with your authorization)**
- De-identified data for algorithm improvement
- Clinical studies (only with explicit consent)
- Medical research contributions

### How We Share Your Information

**Permitted Disclosures (No Authorization Required)**

We may disclose your PHI without authorization for:

1. **Treatment, Payment, and Operations**: As described above
2. **Required by Law**: Court orders, subpoenas, regulatory requirements
3. **Public Health Activities**: Disease reporting, FDA notifications
4. **Health Oversight**: Audits, investigations, inspections
5. **Law Enforcement**: When legally required
6. **Serious Threats**: To prevent serious harm to individuals or public
7. **Workers' Compensation**: If you file a claim
8. **Coroners and Medical Examiners**: For death investigations
9. **Organ Donation**: To organ procurement organizations

**Disclosures Requiring Authorization**

For all other purposes, including:
- Marketing communications
- Sale of PHI
- Psychotherapy notes (if applicable)
- Most research purposes

We will obtain your written authorization before disclosing PHI.

### Your Rights Under HIPAA

**1. Right to Access**
You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your PHI within 30 days of request.
- **How to Request**: Submit written request to [privacy officer email]
- **Format**: Electronic (preferred) or paper copy
- **Cost**: Reasonable, cost-based fee may apply

**2. Right to Amend**
You may request amendments to your PHI if you believe it is incorrect or incomplete.
- **How to Request**: Submit written request with supporting documentation
- **Timeline**: We will respond within 60 days
- **Denial**: If denied, we will provide written explanation and your right to submit a statement of disagreement

**3. Right to Accounting of Disclosures**
You may request a list of certain disclosures we made of your PHI in the last 6 years.
- **Exclusions**: Treatment, payment, operations, and disclosures to you
- **How to Request**: Submit written request
- **First Request**: Free; subsequent requests within 12 months may incur reasonable fee

**4. Right to Request Restrictions**
You may request restrictions on how we use and disclose your PHI.
- **Not Required**: We are not required to agree, except for disclosures to health plans for services you paid for in full out-of-pocket
- **How to Request**: Submit written request specifying restrictions

**5. Right to Confidential Communications**
You may request we communicate with you in a specific way or at a specific location.
- **How to Request**: Submit written request with alternative contact method
- **Reasonable Requests**: We will accommodate all reasonable requests

**6. Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice**
You may request a paper copy of this Privacy Policy at any time.

**7. Right to Opt-Out of AI Analysis**
You have the right to opt-out of AI-powered analysis while still receiving standard care.

### Security Safeguards

We implement comprehensive technical, physical, and administrative safeguards:

**Technical Safeguards**
- Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Multi-factor authentication for all users
- Automatic session timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity
- Comprehensive audit logging of all PHI access
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing

**Physical Safeguards**
- Secure data centers with 24/7 monitoring
- Restricted physical access to servers
- Secure disposal of physical media
- Workstation security policies

**Administrative Safeguards**
- HIPAA training for all employees
- Background checks for workforce members
- Incident response and breach notification procedures
- Business Associate Agreements with all third parties
- Regular risk assessments

### Data Retention and Deletion

**Retention Period**
- Active patient records: While patient is active
- Inactive records: 7 years after last encounter (minimum)
- Minor records: Until age of majority + 7 years
- Audit logs: 6 years minimum

**Secure Deletion**
- Data wiping (not just flagging)
- Certificate of destruction provided upon request
- Retention of minimum necessary data for legal/regulatory compliance

### Breach Notification

In the event of a breach affecting your PHI:
- **Timeline**: We will notify you within 60 days of discovery
- **Method**: Written notice by mail or email
- **Content**: Description of breach, types of information involved, steps we're taking, what you can do
- **Regulatory Notification**: We will also notify the Department of Health and Human Services as required

### Changes to This Policy

We reserve the right to change this Privacy Policy. Changes will be effective for all PHI we maintain, including information created or received before the change.
- **Notification**: We will post the revised policy on our website and make it available upon request
- **Effective Date**: Changes become effective on the date of posting

## 5. Breach Response Plan

### HealthPulse Pro Breach Response Plan

**Purpose**: Establish procedures for responding to breaches of unsecured PHI

**Scope**: All workforce members, business associates, and third parties handling PHI

### Definitions

**Breach**: Unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises the security or privacy of the information

**Unsecured PHI**: PHI that is not encrypted or destroyed according to HIPAA standards

**Discovery**: First day on which breach is known or should have been known

### Breach Response Team

**Incident Commander**: Chief Privacy Officer  
**Security Officer**: Chief Information Security Officer  
**Legal Counsel**: [Law firm/attorney name]  
**Communications**: Director of Communications  
**Technical Lead**: Chief Technology Officer  
**Clinical Lead**: Chief Medical Officer

**Contact Tree**: [Emergency contact numbers for all team members]

### Response Procedures

**Phase 1: Detection and Containment (0-2 hours)**

1. **Immediate Actions**
   - Stop the breach if ongoing
   - Secure affected systems
   - Preserve evidence
   - Document timeline

2. **Initial Assessment**
   - Identify affected PHI
   - Estimate number of individuals
   - Determine cause of breach
   - Assess ongoing risk

3. **Notifications**
   - Alert Incident Commander
   - Assemble Breach Response Team
   - Initiate communication protocol

**Phase 2: Investigation (2-24 hours)**

1. **Detailed Analysis**
   - Forensic investigation (engage external firm if needed)
   - Identify all affected individuals
   - Document types of PHI exposed
   - Determine breach timeline
   - Assess probability of compromise

2. **Risk Assessment**
   - Nature and extent of PHI
   - Unauthorized person who accessed PHI
   - Whether PHI was actually acquired/viewed
   - Extent to which risk has been mitigated

3. **Documentation**
   - Incident report
   - Evidence collection
   - Chain of custody
   - Interviews with involved parties

**Phase 3: Notification (within 60 days of discovery)**

1. **Individual Notification** (without unreasonable delay, max 60 days)
   
   **Method**:
   - Written notice (first-class mail or email if previously agreed)
   - Substitute notice if contact information insufficient:
     - Web posting for 90 days (if >10 individuals)
     - Major media notice (if >10 individuals in same area)
   
   **Content** (plain language):
   - Brief description of breach
   - Types of PHI involved
   - Steps individuals should take
   - What we're doing to investigate and mitigate
   - Contact information for questions
   
   **Template**: See Individual Notification Letter Template below

2. **HHS Notification**
   
   **If affecting 500+ individuals**:
   - Notify HHS Secretary within 60 days
   - Method: Online at https://ocrportal.hhs.gov/ocr/breach/wizard_breach.jsf
   
   **If affecting <500 individuals**:
   - Maintain log
   - Submit annually (within 60 days of calendar year end)

3. **Media Notification** (if affecting 500+ individuals in same jurisdiction)
   - Prominent media outlets
   - Same timing as individual notification
   - Same content as individual notification

4. **Business Associate Notification** (if breach occurred at BA)
   - BA must notify CE within 24 hours
   - Follow BA's breach notification procedures

**Phase 4: Remediation (ongoing)**

1. **Immediate Fixes**
   - Patch vulnerabilities
   - Reset credentials
   - Enhance security controls
   - Additional training

2. **Long-Term Improvements**
   - Root cause analysis
   - Process improvements
   - Technology enhancements
   - Policy updates

3. **Monitoring**
   - Enhanced monitoring of affected systems
   - Credit monitoring for affected individuals (if appropriate)
   - Identity theft services (if appropriate)

**Phase 5: Documentation and Reporting (within 30 days)**

1. **Incident Report**
   - Detailed timeline
   - Investigation findings
   - Root cause analysis
   - Individuals notified
   - Remediation steps

2. **Compliance Documentation**
   - HHS notification confirmation
   - Individual notification records
   - Media notification proof
   - Investigation reports

3. **Lessons Learned**
   - What went well
   - What could improve
   - Recommendations for future
